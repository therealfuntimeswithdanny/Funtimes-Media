Is Microsoft 365 worth it ?

Published on April 30, 2025 | #Fontawesome

The value of a subscription can differ greatly depending on an individual's unique requirements. I personally pay $9.99 each month for my Office subscription, and I find it to be a fantastic price for the features and perks it provides.

One of the main benefits of the paid Office subscription is having access to the complete suite of Desktop Apps, as well as the innovative Copilot feature. These tools offer a smooth experience for managing office files directly through file explorer and desktop applications, making tasks quicker and more efficient.

Here's my in-depth review of specific applications:

PowerPoint: I consider PowerPoint to be one of Microsoft's most impressive products, second only to Windows. Its user-friendly design, free from overly rounded corners and complex dropdown menus, makes it enjoyable to use. The intuitive layout helps users create professional presentations effortlessly.

Word: This is likely my favorite and most frequently used Office application. I depend on it heavily for drafting my blog posts, and the integration with OneDrive allows me to access and edit my files from almost anywhere. The built-in Copilot feature is a revolutionary tool; it assists me with grammar checks, ensures information accuracy, and even aids in expanding or condensing my blog content as needed.

Copilot: I am thoroughly impressed with this feature. As a front-end web developer, I primarily focus on the front end, so I don't need AI assistance there. However, for back-end tasks like those involving Cloudflare Workers, Copilot is invaluable. It's not only swift but also exceptionally intelligent, providing deep insights and powerful problem-solving capabilities that enhance my workflow.

Overall, I sincerely believe that the $9.99 monthly fee is worth it. Although there is a chance that they might use personal data for various reasons, the convenience and utility I gain from Word and the other features make it a worthwhile investment for me.
